Hawa Mahal

About Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds", is a famous historical monument in India. It is known for its unique honeycomb design with many small windows. The structure look like a crown and is one of the most beautiful buildings in Jaipur.

Hawa Mahal is located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. The palace is made of red and pink sandstone, which matches the color of Jaipur, also known as the Pink City.

The palace was designed so that royal women could watch street festivals without being seen by the public. The many windows allowed cool air to pass through, keeping the palace comfortable even in hot weather.

Hawa Mahal is an important symbol of Rajasthan's culture and architecture. It attracts many tourists from all over the world and is an important part of India’s history.

Interesting Facts

  • Hawa Mahal has 953 small windows called "jharokhas".
  • It was built without a proper front entrance.
  • The palace is five stories tall.
  • It looks like the crown of Lord Krishna.
